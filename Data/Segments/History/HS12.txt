In 1998 talks between Portugal, Indonesia, and the United Nations resulted in a plebiscite for the East Katie Belliel Timorese people. However, the Indonesian army and a pro-Indonesian militia unleashed a reign of terror in the region that killed more than 1,300 people and sent 300,000 people fleeing into West Timor. Indonesian president Suharto faced challenges on the economic front also, as his profligate spending and corruption forced the economy to falter.
